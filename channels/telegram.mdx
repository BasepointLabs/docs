Channels let an AI employee receive messages outside the Praxic app. Telegram is the primary customer-facing inbound channel exposed in the API.

## How Telegram works

A Telegram connection links an AI employee to a Telegram bot or chat configuration. Incoming Telegram messages are handled by the channel provider and routed to the assigned AI employee.

## Allowlist controls

Use allowlists to control who can send messages to the employee. Keep the allowlist narrow for production agents.

## Common setup flow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create or choose an AI employee">
    Make sure the employee has the right instructions, tools, and knowledge.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect Telegram">
    Add the Telegram channel configuration for the workspace.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the allowlist">
    Restrict who can message the employee.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send a test message">
    Verify the employee answers with the expected knowledge and tone.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## API endpoints

| Method | Path | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `GET` | `/channels` | List connected channels |
| `POST` | `/channels/:channel` | Connect a channel, such as Telegram |
| `PATCH` | `/channels/:id` | Update channel allowlist settings |
| `DELETE` | `/channels/:id` | Disconnect a channel |

<Note>
  Provider webhook endpoints are public and protected by provider-specific secret token checks. You usually do not need to call those webhook endpoints directly.
</Note>
