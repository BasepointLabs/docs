Use this guide to move from a new workspace to a working AI employee.

## Before you begin

You need:

- A Praxic account with access to a workspace
- A clear job for the AI employee, such as handling leads, drafting content, or answering customer questions
- Any documents, website URLs, or third-party accounts you want the AI employee to use

## 1. Create or choose an AI employee

Start from **AI employees** and create a new employee. Give it a clear role name, such as `Sales assistant`, `Support researcher`, or `Content drafter`.

A strong setup includes:

- **Name:** What teammates will call the AI employee
- **Description:** A short summary of what it does
- **Instructions:** The operating rules, tone, boundaries, and desired output format
- **Provider and model:** The model family the AI employee should use
- **Status:** Active employees can be used in chat, workflows, and channels

<Note>
  If you are not sure which model to use, keep the default. You can review available models in **Settings** or through the models API.
</Note>

## 2. Add the tools it can use

Tools let an AI employee take action. Examples include sending emails, reading connected apps, creating issues, researching leads, and working with knowledge bases.

Only enable tools the employee needs. This keeps responses focused and reduces mistakes.

## 3. Add knowledge

Create a knowledge base, then add documents by pasting text or adding a website URL. Assign the relevant documents to the AI employee.

Good knowledge sources include:

- Product FAQs
- Sales playbooks
- Policy documents
- Help center pages
- SOPs and internal notes that the AI employee is allowed to use

## 4. Connect integrations

Connect accounts once at the workspace level, then allow specific AI employees or workflows to use them.

Common setup paths:

- Connect native integrations such as Linear or Google where available
- Connect Composio toolkits to unlock app-specific actions
- Add API keys for providers that require them
- Configure inbound channels such as Telegram for customer or team conversations

## 5. Test in chat

Open the employee and send a realistic request. Ask it to explain what information it used and what action it would take next.

Good first prompts:

- `Summarize what you know about our refund policy.`
- `Draft a reply to this lead and tell me what data you used.`
- `Create a short task list for onboarding a new customer.`

## 6. Automate repeated work

Once chat works, turn repeatable steps into a workflow. Workflows can run on a schedule, event, webhook, or agent trigger.
